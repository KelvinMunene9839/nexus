About Us

6 young Rwandans who refused
to write a report.

We built a movement instead. Nexus Afrika Movement is a cell-based job creation engine powered by art, culture, design and innovation — born at ALU, built for Rwanda, spreading across Afrika.

NAM was born out of a simple, urgent observation: Rwanda has 646,000 jobless people — mainly young people — sitting on a goldmine of creative talent with nowhere to go. Imigongo art, Agaseke baskets, Kinyarwanda storytelling — these are globally unique assets that generate almost zero income for the youth who carry them.

We are six ALU students who decided that wasn't acceptable. Instead of writing an academic report about the problem, we designed a self-replicating, cell-based solution. NAM starts in a single Kigali cell — a Cell Executive, a free venue, and 30 unemployed youth. Four days of training in Art, Culture, Design & Skills. Then youth start earning real income through products, shops, markets, and commissions.

The movement doesn't stop there. Trained local leaders continue the work after NAM leaves. Each cell trains the next. Kigali in 3 months. Rwanda in 15 months. Afrika by Year 2. NAM is the bridge between talent and income.

"Only 5% of talented Rwandan youth ever get the opportunity to develop their gifts."

— Brian Iisugwe, Policy & Communications Coordinator, Root Foundation · Stakeholder Interview, 2026

Vision & Mission

We are not a project. We are not a program. We are a movement — and movements don't stop.

🔭

Our Vision

An Afrika where every young person with a creative gift has a real economic pathway — where art, culture, design and innovation are recognised as primary engines of youth employment across the continent.

🎯

Our Mission

To create 3,600 jobs in Kigali through a cell-based training movement, then replicate across Rwanda and Afrika — using creative skills, local selling platforms, and a self-sustaining commission model.

🔁

How We Work

We enter a cell, train 30 youth in 4 days, appoint local leaders, connect them to selling platforms, and move to the next cell. The movement grows itself. NAM earns commission. Youth earn income. Culture is preserved.

Our Journey

From a classroom conversation at ALU to a structured movement — here's how NAM came to life, what challenged us, and what we learned.

Early 2026 · The Spark

Born in a Classroom

Six ALU students kept returning to the same question: why do 646,000 Rwandans remain jobless while sitting on extraordinary creative assets? That frustration became the foundation of NAM.

🌱 Origin
E-Lab Entry · Defining the Model

Building the Cell Model

We entered ALU's E-Lab and spent weeks designing our self-replicating cell approach. The insight: don't train youth once and leave — train local leaders who keep the movement alive permanently.

⚡ Breakthrough
Research Phase · Stakeholder Interviews

Validating the Problem

We conducted stakeholder interviews across Kigali. Brian Iisugwe of the Root Foundation confirmed: "Only 5% of talented Rwandan youth ever get the opportunity to develop their gifts." That number broke our hearts — and sharpened our resolve.

🏆 Validation
Solution Design · The 4 Pillars

The NAM Framework is Born

We designed our four-pillar approach: Nexus Training, Nexus Grooming, Nexus Competition, and Nexus Explosion. Each stage takes youth from zero to earning to showcasing — in one continuous pipeline.

How NAM Sustains Itself

NAM is not dependent on grants. It earns its own revenue from day one — through six independent income streams that grow as the movement grows.

🛒

Marketplace Commission

Small % on every product sold through NAM-connected platforms. Scales automatically as more youth join and sell.

🔥

Stove Sales

NAM-designed pallet stoves sold to families and communities — clean energy income that also fights deforestation.

📚

Book Sales

Cultural books sold locally and globally — every copy preserves Rwandan history and earns revenue for the movement.

🎪

Nexus Explosion

Event sponsorships and investor access fees from our grand creative showcase — the biggest creative economy event in Kigali.

🤝

Brand Partnerships

Companies access NAM's growing network of skilled, branded young creatives across Rwanda and beyond.

🌿

Environmental Grants

NAM qualifies for both youth employment AND climate funding — double eligibility for international grant programs.

Research Analysis

The Evidence Behind NAM

We didn't guess. We researched — stakeholder interviews, global precedents, and on-the-ground validation in Kigali.

"Only 5% of talented Rwandan youth ever get the opportunity to develop their gifts."
— Brian Iisugwe, Policy & Communications Coordinator, Root Foundation · Stakeholder Interview, 2026

🌍 Global Proof

South Korea's K-Pop, Nigeria's Nollywood, and Colombia's Medellín transformation show that creative economies can drive national youth employment at scale. Rwanda has the assets. NAM provides the structure.

🎨 Rwanda's Cultural Assets

Imigongo art, Agaseke baskets, and Kinyarwanda storytelling are globally unique and in international demand. These are world-class creative assets with almost no monetization infrastructure currently supporting them.

🛒 Platform Gap

Local Rwandan selling platforms exist but are invisible and underused. Talented youth cannot reach buyers. NAM bridges this gap by branding youth and connecting them directly to these platforms with trained selling skills.

🌿 Environmental Need

Wood charcoal burning is a leading cause of deforestation and climate pollution in Rwanda. NAM's pallet stove programme — made from sawdust and leaves — directly addresses this crisis as a core part of our creative economy model.
